The Story

La Madame

Haitian to her core, raised on nerve. De Madame moves through film and music with the composure of a woman who has never once asked for permission.

She calls herself LaMamiWata — after the water spirit of beauty, wealth and power that runs through Haitian and West African lore. It fits. There is something tidal about her presence: calm on the surface, undeniable underneath. An Aries with, by her own admission, no patience for anything less than the life she's building.

Between Dallas and Mississippi she works the screen and the stage in equal measure — a Creole tongue, a femme-fatale gaze, and a discipline that treats her own name like an asset to be protected.

“Mwen bèl. Belle Âme.”
Haitian Creole & French — “I am beautiful. Beautiful soul.”

“Carrying myself like the investment I am.”
